Zometool Tetrahedra Challenge

Solutions to the 'Tetra Challenge' on page 5 of the Zometool manual

Select a row in the table to see the tetrahedron. Rows marked with an asterisk (*) represent chiral tetrahedra; they have both left-handed and right-handed forms.

Use your mouse to manipulate the 3D view: left button to rotate, right button to pan, and scroll gesture to zoom.

The number of balls shown is copied from the Zometool manual. Several of the tetrahedra use more balls than just the 4 at their vertices. The manual calls for #2 and #8 to use 5 balls, but they actually require only 4 balls. One ball can be omitted by replacing the R1+R2 or B1+B2 edge with the equivalent R3 or B3 strut.

In all other cases where more than 4 balls are shown, the additional balls are actually necessary because they join two struts of the same length to produce edges that are twice as long as a standard strut.
